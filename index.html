About the score

The score estimates the probability that a patient undergoing middle meningeal artery (MMA) embolization for chronic subdural hematoma will go on to require rescue surgery (open burr-hole evacuation or craniotomy) after the index procedure.

Three pre-procedural integer scores are available:

Model 1 — Full (7 pts)

Six variables including anticoagulation, platelets, antiplatelet therapy, and anterior + posterior embolization. AUC 0.704 (optimism-corrected). Recommended cutoff ≥ 4.

Model 3 (5 pts)

Four variables — keeps platelets and antiplatelet therapy, drops anticoagulation and the procedural variable, age stratified at >85 instead of >80. AUC 0.695. Useful when the procedural plan is not yet decided.

Model 2 — Simple (4 pts)

Three variables: age, SDH volume, anticoagulation. No laboratory values, no procedural plan needed. AUC 0.636. Designed for triage where lab and procedural data are unavailable.

All three are calibrated across deciles of predicted probability (Hosmer–Lemeshow P > 0.5), so the absolute risk numbers can be used directly. The integer scores outperform random forest, gradient boosting, and XGBoost trained on the same inputs at this event count.
