About the score

The score estimates the probability that a patient undergoing middle meningeal artery (MMA) embolization for chronic subdural hematoma will go on to require rescue surgery (open burr-hole evacuation or craniotomy) after the index procedure.

Four pre-procedural models are available — three integer scores and one continuous data-driven model:

Model 1 — Full (8 pts)

Seven variables including anticoagulation, focal deficit, platelets, antiplatelet therapy, and anterior + posterior embolization. AUC 0.69 (optimism-corrected). Recommended cutoff ≥ 5 (10.4% vs 38.0% rescue, 3.7-fold gap).

Model 3 (6 pts)

Five variables — keeps platelets, antiplatelet, and focal deficit; drops anticoagulation and procedural plan. Age stratified at >85 instead of >80. AUC 0.68. Recommended cutoff ≥ 4.

Model 2 — Simple (5 pts)

Four variables: age, SDH volume, anticoagulation, focal deficit. No laboratory values, no procedural plan needed. AUC 0.62. Designed for triage where lab and procedural data are unavailable.

Model 4 — Data-driven

Lasso-selected continuous logistic model (7 predictors): age, platelets, hypertension, antiplatelet, gait, anterior + posterior, SDH volume ≥100 mL. 5×10 CV AUC 0.67 (95% CI 0.58–0.76). Sensitivity benchmark — confirms the integer scores are not sacrificing discrimination.

All integer models are calibrated across deciles of predicted probability (Hosmer–Lemeshow P > 0.5), so the absolute risk numbers can be used directly. The integer scores match the data-driven Model 4 and outperform random forest, gradient boosting, and XGBoost trained on the same inputs at this event count (CV-AUC 0.57–0.61 for ML).

Note (May 2026): the focal-deficit variable was re-included after a coding correction in the source data raised its prevalence from 26.2% to 59.8%, in line with published MMA-embolization series.
