About the score

The score estimates the probability that a patient undergoing middle meningeal artery (MMA) embolization for chronic subdural hematoma will go on to require rescue surgery (open burr-hole evacuation or craniotomy) after the index procedure.

The score family is structured in two tiers — two primary knowledge-driven scores designed for clinical use, and three sensitivity models for methods comparison:

Primary

Model 1 — Full (7 pts)

Six variables: age, SDH volume ≥100 mL, anticoagulation, platelets <150 ×10⁹/L, antiplatelet therapy, and anterior + posterior embolization. AUC 0.70 (optimism-corrected). Recommended cutoff ≥ 4 (8.6% vs 36.5% rescue, 4.2-fold gap).

Model 2 — Simple (4 pts)

Three variables: age, SDH volume, anticoagulation. No laboratory values or procedural plan needed. AUC 0.64. Designed for triage where lab and procedural data are unavailable.

Sensitivity

Model 3 (5 pts)

Co-author parallel derivation. Four variables — age (cutoff >85), SDH volume, platelets, antiplatelet. AUC 0.70. Useful when the procedural plan is not yet decided.

Model 4 — Data-driven

L1-penalized (lasso) logistic regression on the full 25-variable candidate space, capped at the EPV-5 maximum of 7 predictors. Lasso selected age, platelets, hypertension, antiplatelet, and SDH volume ≥100 mL. 5×10 CV AUC 0.66 (95% CI 0.56–0.75). Class-imbalance sensitivity (none / class_weight=balanced / SMOTE) gave overlapping AUCs (0.65–0.67), confirming the small event count drives the ceiling rather than imbalance per se.

Focal-deficit variants

Toggle "Sensitivity sub-analysis: include focal deficit" above to switch to the focal-included Model 1, 2, or 3. After a data-coding correction (prevalence 26.2% → 59.8%), focal deficit was non-significant in multivariable analysis (M1 adjusted OR 0.80, P=0.58). AUCs differ by <0.02 from primary; the variable was excluded from primary scores for parsimony.

Model 5 — Enhanced sensitivity bundle

Methodological-stress sensitivity for Model 4. Bundles seven enhancements: restricted cubic splines on continuous predictors, prespecified clinical interactions, Optuna-tuned elastic-net, stacked ensemble (logistic + GBM), Bayesian logistic regression with informative priors anchored to published MMA series, MICE imputation for missing SDH volumes, and a richer continuous-feature set. Composite CV-AUC 0.63 (0.52–0.74); Bayesian-with-priors variant CV-AUC 0.67 (0.57–0.77). None of the enhancements moved AUC outside the Model-4 confidence interval — confirming that the small event count (n=36) is the binding constraint, not method choice. Reported as text-only sensitivity; not used clinically.

All primary models are calibrated across deciles of predicted probability (Hosmer–Lemeshow P > 0.5), so the absolute risk numbers can be used directly. The integer scores match the data-driven Model 4 and outperform random forest, gradient boosting, and XGBoost trained on the same inputs at this event count (CV-AUC 0.57–0.65 for ML).
